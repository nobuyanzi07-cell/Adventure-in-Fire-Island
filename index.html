The Story

The Wildernesss

Fire Island is a narrow barrier island along the shore of Long Island. No city noise beyond the wind through pitch pine and the crash of Atlantic ocean surf beyond the dunes. It is surreal

The highlight of the trip was walking the Sunken forest. Just minutes away the beach opens wide and empty and is protected as a National Seashore since 1964. I watched crabs darting acrosss the beach. It was one of those rare places that feel genuinely unimproved

The communities along the island each ahd their own character. Cocktails and cold root beers after long walks, vibrant nightlife and every return trip reveals a different version of it.
